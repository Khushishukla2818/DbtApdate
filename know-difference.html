What is Aadhaar Linking?

üîó

Aadhaar linking is the foundational process of connecting your unique 12-digit Aadhaar number with various government and financial services to enable seamless, paperless, and cashless transactions.

What is Aadhaar?

Aadhaar is a 12-digit unique identity number issued by UIDAI (Unique Identification Authority of India) based on biometric and demographic data of residents.

Common Aadhaar Linking Examples:
üè¶
Bank Account Linking

Essential for receiving government subsidies, scholarships, and benefits directly in your account

üì±
Mobile Number Linking

Required for OTP-based authentication and mobile-based services

üìã
PAN Card Linking

Mandatory for income tax filing and financial transactions above certain limits

üéì
Scholarship Portal Linking

Required for scholarship registration and verification on National Scholarship Portal

Key Benefits:
  • Single identity proof for multiple services
  • Reduced paperwork and faster processing
  • Enhanced security and fraud prevention
  • Direct benefit transfer without intermediaries

What does DBT Enabled Mean?

üí∞

DBT (Direct Benefit Transfer) Enabled status means your bank account is registered and verified to receive government subsidies, scholarships, and benefits directly from government treasuries without any intermediaries.

How DBT System Works:
1
Eligibility Verification

Government verifies your eligibility for schemes using Aadhaar authentication

2
Bank Account Validation

Your Aadhaar-linked bank account details are validated for transfer

3
Direct Transfer

Amount is transferred directly from government treasury to your account

4
SMS Confirmation

You receive SMS confirmation about the credit in your account

What is Aadhaar Seeding?

üå±

Aadhaar Seeding is the critical process of mapping your Aadhaar number with your bank account in the bank's core banking system, specifically enabling your account to receive Direct Benefit Transfers (DBT) from government schemes.

Seeding vs. Linking - Key Difference:
Aspect
Aadhaar Linking
Aadhaar Seeding
Purpose
General identification & authentication
Specific for government benefit transfers
Process
Connecting Aadhaar with services
Mapping Aadhaar in bank's CBS system
Result
KYC compliance, identity verification
DBT eligibility, scholarship transfers
How to Complete Aadhaar Seeding:
üè¢
Bank Branch Visit
  • Visit your bank branch with original Aadhaar card
  • Fill Aadhaar seeding request form
  • Submit to bank official for processing
  • Get acknowledgment receipt
üíª
Online Banking
  • Login to your bank's online portal
  • Navigate to 'Aadhaar Seeding' section
  • Enter your 12-digit Aadhaar number
  • Verify OTP and submit request
üì±
Mobile Banking
  • Open your bank's mobile app
  • Go to 'Services' > 'Aadhaar Seeding'
  • Enter Aadhaar number and verify
  • Confirm seeding request
üèß
ATM Method
  • Visit bank ATM with your debit card
  • Select 'Services' > 'Aadhaar Services'
  • Choose 'Aadhaar Seeding' option
  • Enter Aadhaar number and confirm
